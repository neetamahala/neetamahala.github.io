Neeta Mahala

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign. Advised by Prof. Erol Tutumluer, my research focuses on utilizing quarry by-products for designing Otta seal–based pavements. Otta seal is a form of bituminous surface treatment for low-volume roads that offers a sustainable, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly road surfacing solution.

Prior to joining UIUC, I worked at the National Highways Authority of India (NHAI), Government of India, from 2015 to 2024, where I served in multiple roles including Deputy Manager (Technical), Manager, and Deputy General Manager. I was responsible for supervising and monitoring large-scale highway infrastructure projects, including both brownfield upgrades and greenfield expressway construction. My work involved overseeing critical project components such as pavements, bridges, rail overbridges, underpasses, and highway safety infrastructure, while ensuring compliance with technical specifications, project timelines, and cost estimates. I also contributed to road safety audits and implemented corrective measures at accident-prone locations. In addition, I served on tender evaluation and procurement committees for major highway development projects under BOT, EPC, and HAM delivery models, gaining extensive experience in infrastructure procurement and project management. During my tenure, I contributed to nationally significant projects including the Delhi–Mumbai Expressway, Amritsar–Jamnagar Expressway, Jaipur Ring Road, and Jodhpur Ring Road.

I earned a Bachelor of Technology in Civil Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, India

Research Interests

My interests lie in sustainable transportation infrastructure and data driven pavement engineering. I am particularly interested in applying statistical modeling, machine learning, and deep learning techniques including computer vision to problems in pavement materials, infrastructure monitoring, and performance evaluation. By integrating experimental research with data driven methods, I aim to contribute to more resilient, efficient, and sustainable transportation systems.

[Dataset] Gridded Daily PM2.5 at 1 km × 1 km Resolution from 2005–2024: Ghana
Abhishek Anand*, Joe Amooli, Daniel Westervelt
Dataset published on Zenodo ( doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17920004 )
More project details available at Columbia University GRASP website

This dataset provides daily estimates of surface PM2.5 concentrations across Ghana at a high spatial resolution of 1 km × 1 km. The data are generated by integrating satellite observations, ground-level monitors, and machine learning models, providing consistent spatiotemporal coverage over 20 years (2005-2024).
